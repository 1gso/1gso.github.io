The stack that rewrites itself as the world rewrites itself
[ tap to reveal ]
Pervasive Adaptive Inference is Rubedo's philosophical commitment and the defining property of an AI-native stack: every model in the system continuously re-parameterizes itself conditional on evolving conditions. Not a single regime detector handing signals downstream — rather a principle running through every layer.

Factor loadings re-weighted as cross-sectional structure shifts. Sparse dynamical systems refit as market equations of motion evolve. Eigenspectra re-estimated as temporal dynamics rotate. Optimization surfaces reshaped as regimes cycle. Copulas, priors, feature transforms, even loss functions — all continuously updated, not as scheduled retraining but as the ongoing operation of the system.

Every strategy interprets the world through this principle, in its own mathematical language: Stat Arb re-identifies cross-sectional dynamical structure; Factor Investing rotates its conditional forecasts; Trend Following retunes its change-point machinery; Macro reshapes its optimization surface. Same philosophy, four distinct mathematical incarnations.
PAI is not a single module. It is the pervasive property of a stack where learning, calibration, and inference happen at every level continuously. What distinguishes PAI from ordinary retraining is the coupling — how updates at one level shape priors at another — and the meta-learning governing which parameters respond fast and which respond slow. This coupling is the firm's intellectual property.

Stat Arb is Rubedo's open research frontier — pattern-based alpha that refuses to fit in the factor taxonomy. Where other firms trade pairs, Rubedo trades implicit dynamical peer groups discovered directly from the data.

SINDy — sparse identification of the market's own equations of motion across the cross-section. Stocks sharing dynamical modes form peer groups no industry code could reveal. Residuals from fitted dynamics become tradeable signal.

LLM-derived peer groups — language-model analysis of earnings calls and business-process language constructs a second peer geometry. Where SINDy and LLM agree, the conviction is highest; where they disagree, the disagreement itself is informative.

Mathematical physics — turbulence measures, damped-driven oscillator fits to intraday spreads, wave mechanics applied to order flow. The parameters are the signal, not the prices.

Classical capital-flow cointegration remains as a baseline. But Stat Arb at Rubedo is intentionally unbounded — the strategy bucket where novel structural alpha is discovered.
Φ participates here as the statistical yardstick for residual significance. Financial residuals are fat-tailed and skewed; Φ's (ν, ε) heads provide the likelihood under which SINDy anomalies are evaluated as tradeable versus within normal tail behavior.

Factor Investing at Rubedo is not a linear factor decomposition. It is Φ — a deep conditional forecaster that emits, per stock per period, the full four-moment distribution of expected returns: mean (μ), volatility (σ), skewness (ε), tail-weight (ν).

Four heads sharing a trunk. Each head routed distinct features — options skew feeds ε and ν; quality feeds μ and ν; valuation feeds long-horizon μ; analyst revisions feed short-horizon μ. Embracing complexity means routing features to where they carry signal, not stuffing them into a single linear regression.

Nonlinear factor structure. Time-varying loadings implicit in regime conditioning. Covariance denoising via RMT. Tail dependence captured by Student-t factor copulas. The "factor zoo" dissolves into a coordinated multi-head model with a single likelihood.

Where classical factor investing emits an expected-return vector, Φ emits a distribution. Where classical factor investing relies on a separately estimated covariance, Φ provides the marginals from which a coherent joint distribution is built.
Φ's likelihood is the sinh-arcsinh-skewed Student-t. Training is Bayesian — MCMC or approximate posterior inference on a deep network emitting four clock-invariant, economically meaningful parameters. This is where the bulk of Rubedo's per-stock quantitative research concentrates.

Trend Following
Spectral change-point dynamics
Koopman · EDMD
ε > 0 · ν small
[ tap ]
Classical trend following lives on moving averages and fixed lookback windows — blunt instruments that neither know when a trend begins nor when one ends. Rubedo replaces them with the Koopman operator.

The Koopman framework lifts nonlinear market dynamics into a linear operator acting on observables. Its eigendecomposition reveals the market's natural coordinates — persistent modes, characteristic time scales, the slow structural currents. Trends are eigenfunctions with eigenvalues near unity and growing amplitude. Change points are discontinuities in the eigenspectrum.

No prior hazard rate. No fixed number of hidden states. The spectrum reveals its own structure; regime transitions emerge as spectral bifurcations. Positions align with dominant Koopman modes, scaled by eigenvalue magnitude and eigenfunction amplitude.

Where classical CTAs detect trends after they have formed, Koopman-based trend detection sees the emergence of new modes in the operator spectrum — often earlier, always more structured.
Trend Following does not consume Φ. Its mathematical framework operates on cross-sectional and cross-asset observables through operator-theoretic methods. Candidate architectural commitment under research: the Koopman operator may also underlie the regime-encoding machinery, consolidating two infrastructures into one spectral object.

Macro allocation at Rubedo is conditional portfolio optimization in the most literal sense: expected returns, covariances, constraints, and even the utility functional are functions of regime state. The optimization surface is not fixed — it reshapes as regimes cycle.

No single efficient frontier. A manifold of frontiers indexed by regime, with per-regime parameters estimated through hierarchical Bayesian methods — unconditional priors shrinking rarely-observed regimes toward the global posterior while preserving regime-specific structure where data is abundant.

The solution is not an allocation vector but an allocation policy — a mapping from regime state to positions across a tractable macro universe spanning equity indices, rates, credit, currencies, commodities, and volatility.

Macro's skew signature is intentionally non-stationary: negatively skewed in carry regimes, positively skewed in safe-haven regimes, symmetric in transitions. This flexibility is the feature — Macro is the strategy whose distributional shape is most directly shaped by the prevailing environment.
Macro does not consume Φ. The macro universe (20–50 instruments) is too small for per-instrument four-moment distributional forecasting to be the right tool; the marginal benefit is dominated by getting regime conditioning correct. Macro's tight coupling to the regime-encoding layer makes it the strategy most sensitive to regime-representation quality.
◆ Tail Risk Parity ◆
Stochastic programming under tail-weighted utility
[ tap to reveal ]
Four strategies with four distinct skew signatures do not combine by equal-risk-contribution, nor by mean-variance, nor by any method that treats the body of the distribution as privileged. They combine through Tail Risk Parity.

The principle: risk should be allocated not by its contribution to variance but by its contribution to disutility in the tail — in the exceptional events, the regime transitions, the moments when the book's distribution is most informative about the fund's robustness.

Scenarios are generated over the joint distribution of strategy returns, assembled from strategy-level marginals and a book-level copula with explicit tail dependence — capturing the degree to which strategy drawdowns cluster in crisis regimes. Gaussian dependence is inadequate; Student-t and skewed-t copulas with regime-conditional parameters are the working framework.

Allocations across the four strategies are set under a stochastic programming formulation with tail-emphasizing utility, such that marginal contributions to tail disutility are balanced. The four-strategy lineup is intentionally constructed for skew diversity — Factor's symmetry, Stat Arb's negative skew, Trend Following's positive skew, Macro's regime-dependent asymmetry — so that the book's skew profile becomes shapable by allocation rather than determined by any single strategy.

This is the final transmutation: four strategies with different tail signatures, aggregated under an objective that prices the tail explicitly, producing a book whose resilience is engineered at the distributional level.
Classical risk parity equalizes marginal volatility contributions (Maillard et al. 2010). Tail Risk Parity generalizes this to equalize marginal contributions to a tail-focused utility functional — prudence/temperance-weighted utility, rank-dependent utility with probability weighting that emphasizes tail probabilities, or a custom functional with explicit tail-body asymmetry. The choice is kept open; the structure ensures the remaining decisions are well-posed.
